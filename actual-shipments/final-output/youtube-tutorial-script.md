# YouTube Tutorial Script: Shipments in LumberLinq

## Title

How to Manage Timber Shipments, Inventory, Payments, and Reconciliation in LumberLinq

## Script

Welcome to LumberLinq. In this tutorial, we will walk through the Shipments workflow for timber and logistics teams.

Start from the Shipments list. This page gives your team a central view of BL number, shipment type, shipment date, shipper, consignee, route, transport-unit count, and shipment status.

Use global search to find a shipment by BL number, exporter reference, buyer order number, shipper, or consignee. For a more specific result, use the column filters for BL number, shipper, consignee, and status.

To create a shipment, select New. The shipment screen is organized into tabs so each team can work in the right area without losing context.

The Shipment Details tab captures the shipment type, mode of transport, shipment dates, ports, final destination, Incoterms, vessel, voyage, or road and rail information.

Next, open Consignment Info. This is where users manage the shipment parties: shipper, consignee, notify party, exporter reference, buyer order number, country of origin, and country of destination.

The Transport Units tab connects the shipment to actual timber stock. LumberLinq protects inventory accuracy, so transport units must be received into inventory before they can be assigned to a shipment. Once received, linked transport units appear inside the shipment and in the shipment view.

The Documents tab keeps shipment document numbers and uploaded files together, including BL, invoice, packing list, certificate documents, and other supporting files.

The Financials & Payments tab tracks invoice value, currency, insurance, freight terms, payment terms, due date, payment status, outstanding amount, and payment history. Use Record Payment to enter payment date, mode, reference number, amount, currency, and notes.

The Local Goods & Audit tab includes local tax and delivery fields, shipment status, approved-by information, and remarks.

Use the Shipment View page for review. When transport units are linked, users can inspect each transport unit from the shipment view without editing the shipment record.

From the shipment edit screen, use Export to generate shipment reports. When transport units are linked, the export dialog shows the connected transport-unit count and report options such as format, access level, company logo, photos, UOM row, charts, stats, and watermark.

Shipments are also connected to Inventory. The Inventory Overview shows available, in-process, in-shipment, and shipped stock. The Inventory In/Out ledger shows movement history. The adjustment dialog supports stock correction. Processing runs support timber conversion workflows.

For operational review, use Reconciliation and Inventory reports to compare shipment, stock, and movement information.

Use Share when another party needs controlled access to shipment information. You can create public, protected, or private links, set expiry and access limits, control downloads, and manage document visibility.

When a shipment should no longer be changed, use Lock. Locked shipments show a lock badge and prevent normal editing.

That is the Shipments workflow in LumberLinq: parties, transport units, inventory, documents, payments, export, reconciliation, sharing, and status control in one connected system.
