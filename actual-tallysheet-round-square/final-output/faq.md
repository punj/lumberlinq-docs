# Tally Sheet FAQ

## What is the difference between Round and Square tally sheets?

Round tally sheets use Length and Girth for round logs. Square tally sheets use Width, Thickness, Length, and Pieces for sawn or rectangular timber.

## How many demo records were captured?

The Round demo sheet has approximately 115 log entries. The Square demo sheet has 20 records and approximately 1200 pieces.

## Where are volumes calculated?

Volume columns appear in the tally grid. Round sheets calculate gross/net CBM and CFT from Length and Girth. Square sheets calculate gross/net volume from Width, Thickness, Length, and Pieces.

## What does Settings control?

Settings controls validation rules, unit of measure, allowances, decimal precision, visible columns, and copy behavior such as Copy Previous Length.

## What export formats are available?

The Export menu shows Excel, PDF, Bundle, and Advanced options.

## Can I import tally rows?

Yes. The toolbar includes Import for file-based import and AI Import for image-based extraction. Dialog screenshots were not captured in this run because the production automation session could not open those dialogs reliably.

## What does the status bar show?

It shows row count, gross volume, unsaved rows, save progress, saved state, and ready state.

## Is there a copy row button?

The verified row action buttons are Add Row Below and Delete Row. Copy Previous Length is available as a Settings option and applies when adding rows.

## Can users change visible columns?

Yes. Use Choose Columns in the toolbar or Visible Columns in Settings.

## Are validation errors visible?

Yes. Invalid entries are highlighted and validation examples were captured for both Round and Square flows.
