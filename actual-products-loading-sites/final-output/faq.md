# Products and Loading Sites FAQ

## Products

### What is a Product in LumberLinq?

A Product is a timber catalog item used across LumberLinq workflows. It can represent round logs, sawn boards, sleepers, planks, poles, or other timber products.

### What product information is visible on the list page?

The captured Products list shows product name, formula, species, product code, description, HS code information, and row actions.

### Which product fields are required?

The captured validation state shows that Product Name, Formula, and HS Code are required before saving.

### Which formula should I select?

Select **ROUND** for round logs and pole-style products. Select **SQUARE** for sawn or rectangular timber such as boards, planks, and sleepers.

### Can I search for a product?

Yes. Use the search field on the Products page. The captured flow verifies filtering with `LL Help Demo`, which returned matching product records.

### Can I edit a product later?

Yes. Open the product from the list, update the required or optional details, and save the changes.

### Why does LumberLinq ask for confirmation before deleting a product?

The confirmation step helps prevent accidental deletion. If the product is already used in operational records, deletion may be restricted to protect linked data.

## Loading Sites

### What is a Loading Site?

A Loading Site is a physical place where timber is stored, processed, staged, or loaded. Examples include yards, mills, forests, ports, and warehouses.

### Which loading site fields are required?

The captured validation state shows that Site Name and Country are required before saving.

### What optional details can I store for a loading site?

The captured form includes optional location, contact, operating, capacity, machine, site type, and notes fields.

### Can I search loading sites?

Yes. Use the search field on the Loading Sites page. The captured flow verifies filtering with `LL Help Demo`.

### Can I edit a loading site after creating it?

Yes. Open the loading site from the list, update the location or operational details, and save the changes.

### Why does LumberLinq ask for confirmation before deleting a loading site?

The confirmation step helps prevent accidental deletion. If the loading site is linked to operational transactions, deletion may be restricted to protect history.

## General

### Why should Products and Loading Sites be maintained carefully?

Clean master data reduces duplicate entries, improves search results, supports accurate reports, and helps timber operations teams work faster.

### Who should manage these records?

Typically, operations managers, logistics coordinators, yard supervisors, or administrators maintain Products and Loading Sites.
