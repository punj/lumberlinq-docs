# YouTube Tutorial Script: Products and Loading Sites in LumberLinq

## Title

How to Manage Products and Loading Sites in LumberLinq

## Estimated Length

4 to 6 minutes

## Script

Welcome to LumberLinq.

In this tutorial, we will look at two important master data modules: Products and Loading Sites. These records help timber and logistics teams keep daily operations organized, searchable, and consistent.

First, let us open the Products module.

The Products page gives your team one central catalog for timber products. In the captured screen, we can see the product count, the Add New Product action, and a table with product details such as name, formula, species, product code, description, HS code, and actions.

Products are used across operational workflows, so it is important to keep this list clean. For example, the product list can include round teak logs, sawn oak boards, pine sleepers, hardwood planks, and utility poles.

To find a product quickly, use the search field above the table. Here, the search term filters the list to matching help demo products. This is useful when your catalog grows and users need to find a product by name, product code, species, HS code, or description.

Now let us create a product.

Click Add New Product. Enter the product name, select the formula, enter the HS code, and add optional details such as species, product code, and description.

Use the ROUND formula for round logs and pole-style timber. Use the SQUARE formula for sawn or rectangular timber such as boards, sleepers, and planks.

If required fields are missing, LumberLinq shows validation messages before saving. The captured validation screen confirms that Product Name, Formula, and HS Code must be completed correctly.

To update a product, return to the list, search for the record if required, open the row action, make the changes, and save. This keeps product details up to date for future work.

If a product should be removed, use the delete action. LumberLinq shows a confirmation dialog before deletion. This helps prevent accidental data loss. If the product is already connected to operational records, deletion may be restricted to protect history.

Next, let us open Loading Sites.

Loading Sites are the physical places where timber is stored, processed, staged, or loaded. These can be yards, forests, mills, ports, warehouses, or other locations used by your business.

The Loading Sites list gives your team a searchable view of these locations. You can use it to find sites by name, contact, country, state, city, capacity, machines installed, or notes.

To create a loading site, click the add action. Enter the site name and select the country. Then add optional details such as state, city, address, postal code, contact person, phone, operating hours, capacity, current volume, machines installed, site type, and notes.

The captured validation state confirms that Site Name and Country are required.

To edit a loading site, open it from the list, update the location or operational details, and save. Keeping this information accurate helps yard supervisors, logistics teams, and office users work from the same location data.

If a loading site should be removed, use the delete action. LumberLinq displays a confirmation dialog before deletion. If the site is linked to operational records, deletion may be restricted to protect transaction history.

Together, Products and Loading Sites reduce spreadsheet dependency, prevent duplicate records, and improve visibility across timber operations.

For best results, use clear naming, consistent product codes, accurate HS codes, and complete site information.

That is how to manage Products and Loading Sites in LumberLinq.
