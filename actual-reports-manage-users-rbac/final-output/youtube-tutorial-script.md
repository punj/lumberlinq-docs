# YouTube Tutorial Script - Reports, Manage User, and RBAC

## Opening
In this tutorial, we will look at Reports, Manage User, and Role-Based Access Control in LumberLinq.

## Reports
Start from the Reports menu. LumberLinq includes reports for business partners, products, loading sites, transport units, tally sheets, reconciliation, shipments, finance, inventory, users, and app usage.

Use filters such as date range, trade type, status, currency, and movement type where available. After changing filters, click Generate or Refresh.

Shipment Report gives a practical operations view: volume, pieces, trade type, parties, ports, BL number, transactions, and financial status.

Financial Report focuses on receivables, payables, overdue amounts, currency totals, partner balances, trends, and transaction details.

Inventory Report shows available stock, stock in process, stock in shipments, movement logs, adjustments, and processing runs.

When a report supports downloads, use the Excel or PDF export buttons in the header.

## Manage User
Open Manage User to view company members, status, login type, last active time, and pending invitations.

Click Invite User to invite one or more team members. Enter email addresses separated by commas, spaces, or new lines. LumberLinq validates the emails before sending.

If RBAC is enabled, choose a role template such as Field Operator, Supervisor, Analyst, Manager, or Custom.

## RBAC
Open Role-Based Access Control from settings. When RBAC is enabled, LumberLinq shows total users, configured users, users with no access, average coverage, and each user's permission count.

Click Configure to open Access Rights. From here, apply a role template, expand permission modules, enable or disable permissions, and save changes.

## Closing
Reports help you understand timber operations, finance, inventory, and user activity. Manage User and RBAC help you give each team member the right access for their role.
